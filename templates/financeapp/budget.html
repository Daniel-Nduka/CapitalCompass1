Budgeting Methods Explained

Zero-Based Budgeting

Zero-based budgeting involves allocating every dollar of your income to a specific expense or savings goal until your total income minus total expenses equals zero. This method ensures that you account for all of your income and expenses, helping you to manage your money more effectively.

50/30/20 Budgeting

The 50/30/20 rule is a simple budgeting framework that allocates 50% of your income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings and debt repayment. This method helps you maintain a balanced budget by prioritizing essential expenses while still allowing for discretionary spending and savings.

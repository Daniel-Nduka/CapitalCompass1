Help and Support

Getting Started

Welcome to CapitalCompass! This guide will walk you through the key features of the app.

To begin, Create a budget by navigating to the create budget button on the top left of the sidebar.

You have the option to create a zero-based budget or a 50/30/20 budget. Once you have created a budget, you can start adding transactions and managing your finances.

Budgets

Navigate to the 'Your Budgets' section to manage your budgets. Here, you can add new budgets, view and edit existing ones, and track your financial goals. The interface and functionality slightly vary depending on the type of budget you choose:

Zero-Based Budget

The zero-based budget allows you to allocate every POUND to a specific expense, savings, or investment. At the start of each month, you allocate your income to various categories, ensuring that your total income minus expenses equals zero. This method helps you have full control over where your money goes.

Key features of the zero-based budget include:

  • Create a new category: Create categories for various expenses such as bills, groceries, entertainment, etc.
  • Add Expense: Add expenses for each category.
  • Edit category and Expenses: Modify your budget categories and expenses as needed throughout the month.

50/30/20 Budget

The 50/30/20 budget divides your income into three main categories: 50% for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings and debt repayment. This method provides a simple structure for managing your finances, ensuring that essential expenses are covered while still allowing for discretionary spending and savings.

  • Needs: Allocate 50% of your income to essentials like rent, groceries, utilities, and insurance.
  • Wants: Allocate 30% of your income to discretionary spending like dining out, entertainment, and hobbies.
  • Savings/Debt: Allocate 20% of your income to savings goals, investments, and debt repayment.

Managing Accounts

To view or add accounts, navigate to the 'Accounts' section. You can add new accounts, view current balances, and manage funds.

  • Account Overview: View all your accounts, including Checking, Savings, and Cash accounts, in one place.
  • Account Details: Check the balance, account type, and other details for each account.
  • Account Management: Edit or delete accounts as needed to keep your financial records up to date.

Transactions

The 'Transactions' section helps you manage all your financial activities seamlessly. Here, you can add, edit, and delete transactions with ease.

To add a new transaction, simply click on the 'Add Transaction' button and fill in the necessary details. You can categorize each transaction, add notes, and set the transaction date to keep your records organized.

  • Outflows: These represent expenses, where money is leaving your account. Outflows are typically represented in red. Outflows must be associated with an expense. If an expense is not available, you can create one on the budget page.
  • Inflows: Inflows represent income or any funds coming into your account, helping you track your earnings and deposits. Inflows are typically represented in green.

To edit or delete a transaction, click on the transaction and select the appropriate action.

Account Support

If you encounter any issues or have questions, visit the 'Account Support' section. Here you can submit a support request, and our team will assist you promptly.
